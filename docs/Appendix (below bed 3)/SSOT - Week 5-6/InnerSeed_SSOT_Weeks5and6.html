03 — The flagship: Content Integration Strategy (the Manual)

P2.01What it is Flagship — decided 10 Jul

A combined content marketing strategy and sales development process manual — ISD's proprietary operating asset and the Assignment 1 e-portfolio artefact. Plug-and-play: if followed, the system runs. Stepwise: written per revenue route. Self-improving: evaluation built in as a permanent function. Final name to be confirmed.

P2.02Audiences & revenue routes Structure

Three audiences: Breathers/subscribers; coaches using the app within their own model; coaches facilitating through the app (the two coach models still to be ironed out — see open items). The sales methodology is written per route:

RouteWhat it isStatus
B2CThe app and its sessions sold direct to BreathersConfirmed

B2B — App-ShellCodebase copied out to another breathwork business that owns it outrightConfirmed
White labelFacilitators run the app under their own brand without taking the codeProposed by Ruaraidh — Jeroen's verdict pending
P2.03Strategic principles the Manual answers to Fixed inputs
  • Immersion is the north star — every marketing claim answers to the quality of the session experience itself.
  • Competitive framing — Othership is the primary named competitor within a broad competitive field: Othership is a content platform that gives you a session; InnerSeed Garden is a practice tool that lets you build one. Deliverables acknowledge the wider field; no single rival is declared.
  • The Presence Process (Michael Brown) is the content and sales formula — message architecture is distilled from it before any route-level content strategy is built.
  • Spotify playlist import (D-43) — the standout differentiator; named and marketed, not buried.
  • Brand identity: Root · Rise · Return — brand identity, voice and visuals are fixed existing inputs the Manual references, not a workstream.
  • Design standard — the Manual and all Phase 2 assets are built in the app's light-mode design tokens (the palette of this document).
P2.04The Stack Production toolset

Confirmed instruments: Claude (operating intelligence and agent team) · Higgsfield AI (visual/video generation) · Postiz (publishing — self-hosted, pending Jeroen's confirmation) · MailerLite (email) · Website + Plausible (owned channel and measurement; Plausible installed, activation scheduled). Removed from the Stack: Loom, Gemini, ComfyUI — reasoning held internally, not carried into deliverables.

P2.05Build sequence Agreed order

Stack section → message architecture (Presence Process distillation) → channel diagram → system schematic. Two channel decisions remain open: LinkedIn's role for the coach routes, and a deliberate position on TikTok.
