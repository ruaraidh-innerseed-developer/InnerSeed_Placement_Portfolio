01 — Agreed Project Framework (Fixed)

The following was agreed on 8 June 2026 and is the structure we work to from this point forward. It does not change without a logged decision.

F.01Document Structure — Final Agreed Framework

Four documents total. No more, no less unless a change is logged.

  • Doc 1 — UX Development Manifesto (internal nickname: SSOT) — Weekly UX notes, observations, screenshots, findings and strategy. One living document per week. Primary working document for ISD and source material for university assignments. Renamed 12 Jun 2026, see SL.03.
  • Doc 2 — Brand Book & Marketing Tool — Standalone professional asset. E-portfolio piece.
  • Doc 3 — Reflective Report + CPD Plan — University Assignment 2. 3,000 words. Formal academic submission.
  • Doc 4 — E-Portfolio — University Assignment 1. Built in Canva or Wix. Draws artefacts from Docs 1 and 2. Includes compulsory 500-word placement blog. Status: in motion — evidenced as Doc 1 is built week by week.
F.02Project Split — Professional vs University Framework

70% professional working relationship with ISD. 30% university module I4168. The two are complementary, not the same. Documents serve ISD first and university second — not the other way around.

F.03Project Vision — InnerSeed Garden Framework

InnerSeed Garden is a mindfulness and guided session app. Core purpose: empowering users to integrate positive change into daily life and find present moment awareness. Key theoretical and commercial backbone: Michael Brown's The Presence Process. More sources to be added over time and logged in Section 05 of this appendix.

F.04Appendix Role — Confirmed Framework

The appendix records everything: documents created, links, decisions, frameworks, sources, session summaries, chat logs. It is the project's master background reference — separate from the UX Development Manifesto (Doc 1), which holds the weekly UX findings, screenshots and design work. The appendix is the memory cloud for the project; the Manifesto is the polished weekly output.

D.05Week 2 — UX Research Brief Dev Log

Week 2 focus is regular user experience testing across core app flows. The guiding development philosophy throughout is: keep it simple, supergastric-case functionality — i.e. design for the most natural, lowest-friction path for the everyday user.

Areas to explore and document this week:

  • Preset mode — how does it feel as a regular user?
  • Filter mode — is it intuitive and discoverable?
  • Saving sessions — is the process clear and easy?
  • Creating sessions — what is the experience of building from scratch?
  • Favouriting sessions — does this feel natural?
  • General — anything that feels unnatural, complex or unnecessarily effortful

SL.04Working Session — 12 June 2026 (continued) Session Log

Completed the opening screen five-question audit (Q3 Trust & tone, Q4 Friction, Q5 Return user consideration). Then began Step 1 of the reorganised testing journey — live Preset playback testing using "Ethereal Voyager."

Q3 (Trust & tone): dashboard reads as sleek/intelligent/“game-like” rather than soft and calming — a tonal mismatch for a wellness product (D-06). Hero banner image cuts off abruptly with a hard edge (D-07).

Q4 (Friction): no single obvious path to "experience something" — the dominant CTA pushes toward building, not listening (D-08); left-hand nav labels (Preparation, Session Builder, The Green House) all describe process/stats, not experience (D-09).

Q5 (Return user): layout still feels overly complex for a simple core task; should be usable "almost like a three year old could use it" (D-10).

Preset playback testing (live): attempting to open a Preset led into Builder mode instead, reproducing W1-01 live (D-11). Session cards only clickable via a tiny play icon, not the whole card (D-12). No way back from a Preset player to the dashboard — nav doesn't offer a route home, and the split InnerSeed logo is ambiguous as a home button (D-13, D-14). The player's control panel doesn't visually read as a breathwork session — reinforces W1-05 breathing visualiser idea (D-15). Session metadata line ("21 min · CONTACT · Connect · ♂ Jeroen") is unclear and may be a labelling bug — flagged for developer check (D-16). Transport bar icons (folder, settings, save) appear to be reused from Builder mode and don't make sense in a Preset playback context (D-17).

Overall: findings now total D-01 to D-17. A recurring pattern has emerged of Builder-mode UI and language bleeding into the Preset/playback experience, compounding the "discovery first" issue identified earlier in the week.

SL.04bWorking Session — 13 June 2026 Session Log

Closed out the remainder of Week 2. Tested favouriting and the "Load Session" modal: favouriting works with clear confirmation feedback, but the destination of favourited items is unclear and the modal itself is too small for its contents and mixes content types without mode-awareness (D-18 to D-20). Tracing these findings back to a single cause identified a major structural finding: three separate, disconnected navigation systems (left sidebar, Session Settings modal, Load Session modal) that don't map onto each other or adapt to the active mode (D-21).

Proposed solution: "threefold consistency" — each of the three modes (Preset, Builder, Creator) offers the same three-part structure, adapted to its purpose. Logged as the single highest-impact recommendation arising from Week 2, grounded in Nielsen's usability heuristics, NN/G scrolling research and Tognazzini's "illusion of completeness."

SL.05Working Session — 15 June 2026 Session Log

Closed out the two items carried over from Week 2: the Preset session completion flow and Filter mode. The completion flow could not be confirmed — the progress scrubber is intentionally locked (to discourage starting and stopping sessions and encourage full completion), and no short-duration Preset exists for a quick preview (D-22).

Language and metadata, confirmed and escalated: the Load Session modal was found to display the same three presets (Breathe Deep Loop, Monsoon Vault, Ethereal Voyager) in Dutch in one instance and English in another, confirming the Week 2 language finding (D-03) extends to actual content rather than a page-level translation prompt alone (D-23). The "CONTACT · Connect" metadata anomaly (D-16) was reproduced on a second preset and pinpointed to the player banner template specifically, since the same field renders correctly in the modal's list view (D-24).

Banner and sidebar: the hero banner was found to be clickable (it reopens the session picker) but gives no visual indication of this, and its artwork is not tied to the InnerSeed Garden identity (D-25). The sidebar and full transport bar were found to remain visible throughout playback with no shift to a calmer state (D-26).

Proposed solution — "cinematic mode": a full-screen mode for active playback was proposed — sidebar and transport chrome removed, replaced by a single breathing visual that itself acts as the only play/pause control, with a quiet countdown beneath. Grounded in Hick's Law, calm technology principles (Case, 2015) and cognitive load theory (Sweller, 1988). Offered as a direction for discussion, not a finished specification.

Further findings: "Recent" does not yet appear to register plays (D-27); minor label inconsistency between "Recent" and "Recents" (D-28); a "Green House" sidebar item appears inconsistently across otherwise similar screens, connecting to the structural concern already raised in D-21 (D-29).

Overall: findings now total D-01 to D-29. Builder mode becomes the headline focus for the remainder of Week 3, followed by mobile testing, then groundwork for analytics and human testing ahead of Week 4. A comms/findings integration plan ("the back garden") is flagged pending scope confirmation. No notes yet from the Friday 13 June focus call with Jeroen.

SL.06Working Session — 17 June 2026 Session Log

Ethereal Voyager was played through to completion, resolving D-22. The only on-screen change at completion is the timer resetting and a single "Go To Green House" prompt appearing, with no acknowledgment that the session has ended — experienced as anticlimactic directly after an intensive session (D-30).

This surfaced a major open question: what the Green House is and how it relates to the Garden identity. The two currently read as overlapping, undifferentiated concepts, and the term "Green House" itself carries connotations (enclosed, artificial, evokes "greenhouse gases") that work against the open, natural quality of the Garden brand (D-31). Connects to D-29 (inconsistent appearance of the same nav item).

A written summary of this point was prepared and sent to Jeroen separately, to be raised alongside other items at the Thursday 18 June discussion, 12pm–2pm. The Green House destination itself has not yet been opened or tested — flagged as needed before the naming question can be finalised.

Overall: findings now total D-01 to D-31. All documents (appendix, recovery brief, Week 3 summary, Week 3 Manifesto) updated accordingly.
