02 — The Project

InnerSeed Garden is a mindfulness and guided breathwork web application. Its purpose is to empower users to integrate positive change into daily life and find present moment awareness. The app has three session modes: Preset (ready-made sessions), Builder (custom sessions), and Creator (facilitator mode). Sessions draw on three content pillars: Ambient Music, Guided Breaths, and Guided Voices.

The key theoretical and commercial backbone is Michael Brown's The Presence Process, which informs both the sales philosophy and the app's approach to present moment awareness and conscious integration.

Core development philosophy: keep it simple. Design for the most natural, lowest-friction path for the everyday user. This was reinforced in Week 2 with the explicit principle “Keep It Simple, Stupid” (KISS), and a new emerging design principle: discovery first — the app should show new users what it can do before asking them to choose a mode.

12 Jun 2026 (continued) — Opening screen audit completed and live testing begun: Q3 (Trust & tone) — dashboard feels sleek/intelligent/"game-like" rather than calming, a tonal mismatch for a wellness product (D-06); hero banner image cuts off with a hard edge (D-07). Q4 (Friction) — no obvious single path to "experience something," dominant CTA pushes toward building not listening (D-08); left-hand nav describes process/stats not experience (D-09). Q5 (Return user) — layout still too complex for a simple core task, should be usable "almost like a three year old could use it" (D-10).

Live Preset playback testing then began using "Ethereal Voyager": attempting to open a Preset led into Builder mode instead, reproducing W1-01 live (D-11); session cards only clickable via a tiny play icon (D-12); no way back from player to dashboard, and the split InnerSeed logo is ambiguous as a home button (D-13, D-14); the player's control panel doesn't visually read as a breathwork session, reinforcing the W1-05 breathing visualiser idea (D-15); session metadata line ("21 min · CONTACT · Connect · ♂ Jeroen") is unclear and may be a labelling bug, flagged for developer check (D-16); transport bar icons (folder, settings, save) appear reused from Builder mode and don't fit a Preset playback context (D-17).

Pattern identified: Builder-mode UI and language is bleeding into the Preset/playback experience, compounding the "discovery first" issue from earlier in the week.

Week 3 (15–19 Jun 2026) — Closing out Preset mode: The two items carried over from Week 2 (session completion flow, Filter mode) were revisited. The completion flow could not be confirmed — the progress scrubber is intentionally locked to discourage starting and stopping sessions, and no short-duration Preset exists for a quick preview (D-22). While testing this, the Load Session modal was found to display the same three presets in Dutch in one instance and English in another, confirming the Week 2 language finding (D-03) extends to actual content, not just a page-level translation prompt (D-23). The "CONTACT · Connect" metadata anomaly (D-16) was reproduced on a second preset and pinpointed specifically to the player banner template, since the same field renders correctly elsewhere (D-24). The hero banner was found to be clickable (it reopens the session picker) but gives no visual indication of this (D-25).

Proposed "cinematic mode": The sidebar and full transport bar were found to remain on screen throughout playback with no shift to a calmer state (D-26), prompting a proposed full-screen mode for active playback — sidebar and chrome removed, a single breathing visual in their place, with the visual itself acting as the only play/pause control. Grounded in Hick's Law, calm technology principles and cognitive load theory.

Further findings: "Recent" does not yet appear to register plays (D-27); minor label inconsistency between "Recent" and "Recents" (D-28); a "Green House" sidebar item appears inconsistently across otherwise similar screens (D-29). Running findings log now totals D-01 to D-29.

17 Jun 2026 — Session completion observed, major naming question raised: Ethereal Voyager was played through to completion, resolving D-22. The only on-screen change at completion is the timer resetting and a single "Go To Green House" prompt — no acknowledgment that the session has ended, experienced as anticlimactic directly after an intensive session (D-30). This surfaced a major open question: "Green House" and "Garden" read as overlapping, undifferentiated concepts, and "Green House" itself carries connotations that work against the Garden identity (D-31, connects to D-29). A written summary was prepared and sent to Jeroen separately, to be raised at the Thursday 18 June discussion, 12pm–2pm, alongside other agenda items. Running findings log now totals D-01 to D-31.

Reopening the session picker later showed only one preset with a different description and a relabelled tab, extending the language/content instability already logged (D-35, escalates D-23). Green House was opened for the first time and found to be a genuine stats and community space — "Your Rhythm," a twelve-month activity record, and Progress/Coaches/Events tabs — resolving the structural half of D-31, though the naming question stands (D-36).
